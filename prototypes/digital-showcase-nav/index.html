What changes · what does not

  • Nothing deleted, nothing moved off its route. All 9 destinations keep their existing hrefs — /sell/company/journey, /sell/company, /sell/company/products, /sell/company/projects, /sell/company/categories, /sell/company/spec-library, /sell/microsite, /sell/microsite/ads. Only vendor-shell-vm.ts (presentation config) is edited.
  • Groups mirror the frozen seam. Content ≠ Presentation (Invariant #9 / Golden Rule 4): "Content" = what buyers read; "Publish & Promote" = how it looks and reaches them. The vendor learns the platform's own model from the nav.
  • The guided path gets top billing. The 3-step journey (Overview → Template & Sections → Preview & Publish) is an action, not a destination — a flat, iconed item above the groups, exactly per DS-W0-R1 ("listed first because it is where a vendor starts").
  • Rename "Authoring journey" → "Showcase Builder" (proposed). "Authoring journey" is internal design language; "Showcase Builder" says what the vendor gets. Alternatives considered: "Setup Guide" (implies one-time), "Getting Started" (implies you finish and leave).
  • "View Public Page" gains an external-link affordance (visual only — same destination as today, the page hosting live_url; the vendor side still never constructs a public slug URL).
  • Zero shell changes. NavSection.label and one-level NavItem.children already exist and render exactly this way for Trust / Communication / Business Docs.
